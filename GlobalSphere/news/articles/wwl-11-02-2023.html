What's up at GlobalSphere?

December 7th, 2022.

I am writing this post when it is currently the evening for me. I have just witnessed the end of a war, with Nineveh surrendering. I have wanted to make this post for a long time, but that situation has been "the last straw" and made me write this up.

First, let's talk about the economy. It is in a horrible state right now. We weren't quick enough to nerf some jobs in the beginning of the server, and we didn't really crack down on the usage of autoclicker. Admittedly, I haven't been playing the server a lot at the time, just looking at the discord chat, or flying around looking at people's builds. I, the one making economy decisions, was a bit out of touch with the server.

So then came the first jobs balance changes. They kind of (?) helped. We made 2 other balance changes as of this writing (3 total), with the last one being pretty infamous. I heavily buffed some weak jobs such as enchanter (which had to have its buffs reverted). I also tried to add artificial inflation to the server by increasing costs and money (trying to weaken the purchasing power of the already existing money). This was counteracted by people grinding miner (I had made a change that gives a lot of money for breaking ore, not knowing that people have already stocked up a lot on them).

At this point, I honestly don't know how to fix it. We were making plans for season 3 in a staff group chat, and we were thinking about adding a money limit per day (which can easily be circumvented by alts or account sharing), or adding a battlepass (quite a bit of work, but I kind of liked this solution for its potential in the future). However, lately I have realized that season 3 is far, far away. Players want new things, but do not want a reset. And I understand that, who wants their builds destroyed? It's also not likely for the server to run out of players in the near future either.

An economy reset is not an elegant solution either. Although jobs were broken, people sank hours of their lives to get a virtual Minecraft currency. So hyperinflation to make money worthless or just wiping money won't work either. Especially considering people need money to fund their towns. Money is also supposed to be a form of progress, which doesn't match with the fact that everyone is maxed.

Speaking of being maxed, we'll move on to progression and updates. Within the first month or so of the server, everyone had a max iron armor and tool set. We don't want to add diamonds to be different, and diamonds eventually lead into netherite (which we absolutely do not want). However, with all the new powers being added (talked about later), it seems like iron is being obsolete. Potential solutions include allowing health upgrades of some form or allowing iron items to be smithed.

To prevent players being bored, we have added many things such as movecraft (in the first season), the brewery plugin, and lately, a custom dimension with custom structures whose portal is found by searching for clues at the warps. Within the first hour of release, the portal was found. Many others soon found the portal and absolutely raided the dimension (which isn't a bad thing!) In the new dimension, we added tridents as a drop (roughly 1 in 30 structures have it). However, now that the dimension is raided, we are worried that new players won't want to travel 4000+ blocks to find some loot. However, we can't just randomly add dimensions, as that will get boring very quickly (and we will run out of blocks to give as loot).

The third subject I want to give my thoughts about is huge towns and inactive towns. Huge towns are great when they have people in it. However, some big towns are one person towns, undeveloped, and often owned by alts (such as Constantinople and Nicosia). The prime culprit of this issue is the large map. People want to claim countries, but they are so large! So people need to make large claims, sometimes with multiple accounts. The vast majority of lands are undeveloped. For example, even a super developed town such as Adna only uses 10% of its lands. Why? Because with a large map, it is practically impossible to use a large area.

Again, there is no clear solution to this. Season 3 is not going to happen for a long time, so we cannot do a map reset. Maps also need to be perfectly sized. Large maps will have unused lands and small maps will be clumped up and players will not have land to develop. A potential extreme solution would be uploading a new map and moving player builds (however, I doubt anyone would want that, and even I don't feel good about this solution).

Now, we're nearing the end of this post. In this section I will discuss brewery. We initially added brewery because of players asking us. One player even made a youtube video for the suggestion. So we thought, "hey, this is a fun addition that people can brew a few drinks with for fun!" It turned out to be a lot more than that, even causing a controversy during the recent in-game war. We added more and more drinks, and players didn't brew them. Drinks weren't really special. One drink was whisky, which could give strength 3, but had quite a few downsides. Lately, we introduced teas, which now that I look back upon it, were very OP. Warped tea gave speed 4 with the only downside being nausea, and crimson tea gave strength 4 and fire resistance with the only downside being slowness. I underestimated the power of strength 4, and I overestimated the power of iron armor.

For the tea situation, we could nerf the effects, but it also seems a little unfair that it gets nerfed after one side uses it in a major war. As for all brewery items, we will need to consider how to make each of them special.

So what about the future? Many of these problems don't have very clear solutions, and if someone doesn't come up with one, we'll just have to "deal with it" with a lot of band-aid solutions.

Not to mention the fact that geopolitics servers are seeing a general decline. It is still unknown how GS will survive this, if things will turn around, or if we will evolve to have different modes or something.

But let's end on a high note. What can we do now? How can we make the server more fun for players RIGHT NOW? Well, some members say that they like custom dimensions. Ideas we had are a Saskatchewan/Ohio dimension, or some other custom map. Kodo, an admin, thinks it would be fun if homes and towns were enabled with a world border in our custom dimensions.
